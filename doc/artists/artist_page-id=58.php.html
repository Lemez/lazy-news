Sahotas - Artist Page
Sahotas
Discography  ·  Booking Agency  ·  Similar Music
Biography:
 

Sahotas perform pulsating Bhangra music fused with Reggae, Dance, Rock and Love Ballads. The five-member brother band writes, arranges, produces and performs the music itself. Currently, it is the best selling Bhangra band in the world. 

Among Bhangra artists, The Sahotas have made a significant contribution to the mainstream market by supporting top reggae band Aswad on their UK tour, a string of peak time television appearances, and performances at music festivals.  

The band’s profound knowledge of Bhangra, music and stage presence, began to take form after winning The School Proms, The National Festival of Music for Youth and performing at The Royal Albert Hall in 1987, the most prestigious venue in Great Britain. Other performances included at The Royal Festival Hall &Queen Elizabeth Hall in London's renowned South Bank. Surjit Sahota, Mukhtar Sahota and Raj Sahota created an animation of musical language that landmarked The Sahota Brothers in the British Asian Music Industry at the average age of 19.

Ambition of the brothers led them to endeavor in, Giddha Pao, their first Punjabi album released in 1987, blending Bhangra rhythms with contemporary sounds. As recognizable new talent they were signed to Multitone Records. Creativity, passion and growth called for three more members to The Sahotas, Prakash Sahota, Vijay Sahota and a session bass player.  

Public demand led to Sahota Beat, their second album released in 1988, which enhanced their success even more. The distinct voice of Surjit, became a melodic fantasy that females craved for. Sahota Beat charted at number one in the Bhangra Charts, creating a firm fan following in the UK.  

Drive, exhaustion and ambition led to the release of their third album, Aaja in 1989. It created a new groove that was to influence the whole of the Bhangra Industry. The new style was received so well that the album gained Platinum and Gold Discs. Performing at a multitude of Bhangra gigs, music festivals, peak time television appearances, they cultivated a crowd following and made them world known.  

1990 saw the release of Are you Feeling single. This was an experimental crossover project for the dance market. Two English tracks and "Bhangra Pauna" (remix).
Sahotas' music was used for major television music program series, called Bhangra Beat aired in the UK. 

After such responsive success, the band released its fourth album Ishk, in 1991. Not only did it top the Bhangra Charts once again, but it remained in the Top Ten, for a record breaking five months.

The band's lead singer Surjit Sahota suffered a broken arm in a serious road accident. This news sent shock waves throughout the entire UK Bhangra Music Industry.  
The band went on to receive the "best upcoming band” award at the 1991 UK Asian Pop Awards, where they gave an outstanding performance of the song Urdas Kar De Han (Let's Pray). The Sahotas also won the “Best innovative Band" Award at "The Bhangra Music Awards". 

Thousands of bootlegged copies of the albums Aaja &Ishk were sold in and around Europe &Africa.  

In 1992, The Sahotas were discovered by I.R.S. Records Ltd. Two years after the release of Ishk there was pressure from Britain's Asian community for another album from the band. The Sahotas released the Punjabi single Hass Hogia, in 1993, on the I.R.S. label. Yet again, this topped the charts and took the Bhangra Industry by storm.
Bootlegged copies circulated the markets in India and strangely enough a video of the single soon appeared on all Asian Music TV Channels, with Lip-Sync.  

After noting the recognized following and the musical ambition of The Sahotas, IRS wanted Bhangra to cross over into the English music industry, which led to the release of Out of Reac single 1994, followed by The Right Time album 1995 on I.R.S. Records (EMI).

The Sahotas toured Canada on their International tour of North America where they played to mainstream audiences at music festivals.
Due to the ever increasing support The Sahotas launched their official website on the Internet, www.thesahotas.com. it was probably the first Bhangra band to go on the worldwide web.  

The Sahotas have supported the Goodwill Children’s Village, South India, raising money for children in the poorer regions of India. Believing in World Peace, Love and Unity, the band performed their Urdas Kar De Han (Let’s Pray), the Universal World Prayer song. 

Decade was finally released in the summer of 1997, on one of the upcoming Bhangra labels Kamlee Records Ltd. "Decade" by The Sahotas became the biggest selling album of 1997. Leading to the Best Contribution to Bhangra Music award at the Bhangra Music Awards.  

The Sahotas released Maa da Pyar (Mothers Love), in the loving memory of their late mother who passed away on August 5th 1997. The tribute single contains 2 ballads, 1 which is sung in English. All proceeds from this single go to a charity.  

In 1998 The Sahotas success continued as they received the “best live act” award at two prestigious award shows, "The Movie Bhangra Awards" and "The Official Student Music Awards”.

1999, Envy Entertainment Ltd. exclusively under license to "Times Music" (Times of India) released The Sahotas' Punjabi album Brotherhood.

The Sahotas shot the latest music video in Mumbai's Juhu Beach. In May 2000, Sahotas made their official debut release in India with the album Brotherhood which was re-titled in India as Teri Meri Gal Ban Gayee. The album entered the Indi Pop Charts and spends a staggering seven months in the top ten. 

While they had swayed, swung &shook millions of Bhangra fans across the world, CD’s &singles of Sahotas were largely circulated throughout the Asian sub-continent mostly in pirated form. This definitely changed when Times Music flooded the market with legal copies of Teri Meri Gal Ban Gayee.

The hugely successful promotional tour of India followed in July 2000, this was the biggest tour ever done by a UK Bhangra artist. Playing in major cities including Delhi, Mumbai, Ahmedabad & Bangalore.

The Sahotas are heavily influenced by Reggae, Rock, Dance, Opera music, and all all kinds of World Music. They are the original pioneers of the Bhangra-Reggae fusion movement since the mid 80's.  

All 5 are self-taught musicians. They love music, recording, playing live and touring and would not change it for anything else. Away from music they like spending time with their families and friends.

The Sahotas Music continues to dominate playlists on leading Asian Radio stations. They have produced 6 hit albums performed in Punjabi and 1 in English. Songs such as “Lokan Nu Nach”, "Aaja Aaja Aaja”, “Mahi Di Udeek”, “Sahotas Show Te Jaake", “Hass Hogia”, "Heerie” and more have gone down as Bhangra classics. Lead vocalist Surjit Sahota has co-written over 30 of the band’s songs.

Sahotas’ recent album Revolution was released by one of India’s major record labels, Tips Music, and spent numerous weeks in the Top 10 India Billboard charts. The album is a collection of greatest hits tracks re-recorded by the band.
