Ville Kangas - Artist Page
Ville Kangas
Discography  ·  Booking Agency  ·  Similar Music
Biography:
 
"The intention is always to find whether it is via odd combinations of instruments or sounds, through an unexpected choice of style, or by experimenting with these combinations a new angle naturally."

Ville Kangas (b. 1977), christened Jaakko Wiljami, is undisputably the most respected, original and self-willed musician and composer produced by the folk and world music hatchery in Kaustinen, Finland. It is the violin in the leading role combined with the inimitable style of its player, as heard on his second solo album released in summer 2005, that has made Ville Kangas a sought-after musician on the Finnish music scene.

Ville is a descendant of musical families in Kaustinen through both his parents, and famous ancestors - Wiljami Niittykoski, Aaro Kentala, Markun Joonas - reveal strong "pelimanni" roots. The family includes both the popular folk music group Kankaan pelimannit from the 1970s and the Kangas brothers on the classical music scene. It was with classical music that Ville, too, started his musical career, from which he took his first sabbatical at the age of five. With his godfather Mauno J?rvel? Ville went on his first domestic as well as international tours while he was still a little rascal, and he successfully absorbed all the teachings from older folk musicians.

While he was studying in Kaustinen College of Music, Ville's future values started to dawn on him and he developed an interest in practical musicianship rather than in his studies. Still officially a student at the Sibelius Academy, Ville was carried away by work, so frankly speaking, there was no more time to study. Pushed forward by his philosophies of life fostering versatility and avoiding boredom Ville has traveled from one pursuit to another. JPP and Luna Nova are familiar names to the friends of folk music, and to Ville, significant band experiences.

Perhaps it is his passion for something new that has given old traditions a new twist in Ville's hands time after time. His musical Vihre haltiatar, staged at the Kaustinen Folk Music Festival, inspired the Finnish Music Information Centre to report to the world as follows: "It is precisely in ventures such as this, combining a familiarity with tradition and mind-stretching study, that the prospects for new Finnish folk music look brightest of all."

Ville believes it is this cross-breeding of classical and folk as well as popular music that explains his style of composition that is based strongly on melody. Natural combining of elements is premeditated and intentional. His precious folk-punk band Prusikoukku already crossed the borders in the 90s. This year there is a new project, called Velho, with former Hedningarna singers Tellu Turkka and Liisa Matveinen, that blends Kalevala-style rune singing and rock.

In the Finnish popular music circles, Ville is known as a flexible professional with the ability to adapt. Lately, he has been working not only as a musician but also as a producer. The past few years' projects with Jarkko Martikainen, Ismo Alanko and Jonna Tervomaa, all of whom appear on Ville's first CD Suuri erehdys, tell us about the respect he has outside the folk music genre. All in all Ville has had his finger in the pie on almost 30 different publications, and the number of instruments he has played on them is probably the same.

Being labeled with folk and world music terms seems restricting to Ville. Within these genres, everything should always be based on some particular tradition. This sometimes prevents music from evolving, living its own life, and musicians from mixing styles, without giving it too much thought.

The new album Yoaika, created with his brand-new band, dashes into the world of instrumental music. On the album Ville and his electric violin are joined by Tomi Havinen (drums), Osmo Ikonen (keybords and vocals), Vesa Laasanen (guitar and Irish bouzouki) and Juha-Matti Rautiainen (bass).

This is how Ville ponders upon the theme of the CD and the origin of his music : "...Yoaika (translates 'night time') refers to the familiar feeling one has in the night that time seems to slow down or stop. Your mind wanders around randomly, at times standing still, at times running wildly here and there. The best time to make music is night time."

The style of both the album and the band keeps moving from one thing to another. Yoaika ranges from soft tones through beautiful Scandinavian melodies to New Orleans beats and poppish world music fusion. The basic idea of the album is deliberately simple, even minimalistic at times. Despite the rock and pop feel of the album, Ville believes it will end up in the World Music Section in record stores, just like the previous one.

" ...but what is bloody important here is being natural. Just forcing an Irish bouzouki and an analogue synthesizer together is doomed attempt. - - - To my taste, it is possible to hide behind the difficulty of the music and the virtuosity of playing, maybe at the cost of the music. That is why I wanted to try and make a band record with music as simple as possible."

Biography by Antti Huntus
