Joan Valent - Artist Page
Joan Valent
Booking Agency  ·  Similar Music
Biography:
 

Spanish musician and composer Joan Valent was born in Palma de Mallorca on July 8 1964. He began his musical studies at the age of five, tutored by private teachers, and continued at the Professional Conservatory of Music in Palma de Mallorca. He initiated his composition studies in 1984 in Mallorca, directed by Romanian composer Egicio Petrovich.

In 1987 Valent presented his first musical work "Mort a la paraula" for piano, violin, clarinet and mezzosoprano. He was recommended to move to Barcelona in order to expand his studies with composers Carles Guinovert, Joan Guinjoan and David Padrós. He attended composition, musical analysis, orchestration and instrumentation classes, as well as cello, piano and orchestral conducting during the following six years.

The following musical works belong to that period: Proporcions Litogràfiques - string quartet; Peça per Orquestra - Orchestra; Kontrapunktum - Piano; Wind Dancers - wind octet; Cuartetos de cuerda 1º y 2º - string quartet; Lacrimae - cello duo; Kosé- Solo cello

With a scholarship from the Balearic regional government, he moved to Los Angeles (California) where he took courses in classical composition, film scoring, and orchestral conducting at UCLA, and, under the guidance of composer Aurelio de la Vega, at the University of Northridge (CSUN). Valent was commissioned several works by National Orchestra of Spain, the IMF in Washington DC and the Chang Foundation.

He began to present his musical works throughout the world, in Japan, Korea, the United States, and Europe. His residence in the United States was extended five years to finalize his additional studies and to work as an assistant to composer Aurelio composer of the Vega. In 1993, Michael Douglas gave Valent the responsibility of creating and premiering "Escape from the century."

The following musical works belong to that period: Passio et Mors - Orchestra; Cuartetos 3º, 4º, 5º - string quartet; Metallum Ballenae - six symphonic percussionists; Neptú I l’infant no nat- Solo percussion + electronics; Escapar del siglo - Soprano, narrator and Orchestra; Intrata -  six percussionists; Deposits - four quartets + electronics; Desideratum - string orchestra

Invited by the Philharmonic Society of Moscow, he moved to that city to work on contemporary composition techniques with the Naidin quartet, tutored by Misha Ravleski, first violin of the Borodin Quartet. Valent was commissioned a cantata for soloists, choir and orchestra by the Balearic Government and other works for the Miró Foundation, violinist Natasha Naidin, cellist Barbara Rönte, the Philharmonic Orchestra of Gran Canaria (Spain), Sa Nostra Chamber Orchestra (Spain), the Elche Orchestra (Spain), and the Dobrich Orchestra (Bulgaria).

Valent began to collaborate as arranger and symphonic composer with artists from other musical genres: Luis Eduardo Aute, Luz Casal, Pedro Guerra, Amancio Prada, José Antonio Ramos, Tam Tam Go, Stephan Micus, Bunbury, Malcom Scarpa, Pablo Guerreo, Los Panchos, Suso Saiz, María del Mar Bonet Sea, Montserrat Caballé, and Joan Manuel Serrat.

The following musical works belong to that period: Piano Concerto - Orchestra + solo piano; 1º Sinfonía - Orchestra; Hastula Regia - Voice , percussion and strings; In Memoriam - Steel Drum + strings; Europa - Solo Violin; StrinG Islands - string orchestra; Sarriá - Voice + strings; Rats Doom - string Orchestra; L’Evangeli segons un de Tants - Orches t ra + choirs + soloists; Europa 2 - Solo cello; Mort d’un poeta - cello Concerto; Hombres Caballos y toros - Percussion, voice + strings; Concierto para timple y Orchestra - solo timple + orchestra; Suite de Mararía - Orchestra

In 1996 Valent relocated to Madrid. He was commissioned works by many orchestras throughout the world and he founded the Ars Ensemble, with which he recorded live the album Ars in April of 2000.

"Ars is a trip to the musical traditions that have populated my life. After living for many years far from my people, immersed in the music of the 20th century, trying to understand, learn and participate like a composer in its history, I returned to Mallorca willing to find my place. I looked for a sound, a poetic feel and a sense to my existence as a composer. It was the poetry, the earth, the proximity of other kinds of music tied to what I proposed until that moment that opened a road for me, a firm sense of what I was looking for.

The poetry of Blai Bonet and his personality led to me Ramón Lluc, a famous philosopher and mystic Mallorcan of the 12th century, and through his work I understood and I understood myself. I returned all of the sudden to structural simplicity, to the rediscovery of melody and admiration for folk music, the voices of my people and my ancestors. From that point was born Ars: an encounter with the Mediterranean where the texts of Ramón Lluc are used to recover the South, the Arab world, the spiritual depth that has always been so bound to our coast, and to the light that floods all those things that in any way bring Turkey to Mallorca, or Tunisia to Naples, much closer.

In Ars, in its different parts, the strings develop that link that binds me to the classical tradition, while the voices, the percussion and the flutes evoke the earth, the history, the verse, the verb. The electronics are a further step forward, yet another part in this communion of music and intentions."

Joan Valent began to write music for films at that time: Súper agentes en Mallorca  -Baleares Films. Directed by M.A. Merino; Delirios de Amor (1998) - Luz de Gas Films. Directed by Toni Capellà; El Conductor (1998) - Índigo Films. Directed by Jorge Carrasco; Mararia (1998)(Orchestra + Arrangements ) - Mararia Films Directed by Antonio Betancor; El Viaje de Arian(1999) - Filmmayer. Directed by Eduard Bosch; Cásate Conmigo Maribel(1999)- Filmmayer. Directed by Ángel Blascos; Demasiado Amor (México, 2000) - Aiete Films. Directed by Ernesto Rimoch; Marujas Asesinas (2001) - Lan Cinema. Directed by Javier Rebollo; After the release of Ars in December of 2000, Joan Valent began to present his music live, and he was nominated for New Music Awards. He performed at several important national and international festivals.

American pop singer David Byrne featured the Ars Ensemble string sextet in his tour of Spain and Portugal in June 2001.

Continuing with his work as arranger and composer, he recorded an album with María del Mar Bonet with his Ars Ensemble- recording 5 concerts (25 to July 30 2001) at the Plaza del Rey (King's Square) in Barcelona.

In April of 2002 Joan Valent's second CD, Ensems, was released. It featured his Ars Ensemble. Several high profile guests participated in the recording: singers Pasion Vega and Teresa Barrientos, piper José Manuel Tejedor, accordionist Joxean Goicoetxea and the album was produced by avant-garde musician Suso Sáiz. It is a delightful musical work in which multiple colors are found: the congenital Mediterranean sensibility that bathes all of his, classicism, minimalism and the creative euphoria free of prejudices.
