Emmett North Jr - Artist Page
Emmett North Jr
Discography  ·  Interview  ·  Booking Agency  ·  Similar Music
Biography:
 
I was introduced to the guitar at age nine by a friend, who taught me a few bars of 'Honky Tonk'. I was again later re - introduced at age twelve where i took more interest in wanting to play because I had written some song poems earlier years before I learned to play.

I was born in a steel town called Middletown, Ohio twenty five from Cincinnati, Ohio south and Dayton, ohio twenty five to the north. My mother's side of the family were more musically inclined, all of my three aunts played piano / organ and sang in the choir. Three of my uncles played instruments one the sax, one the trumpet and one trombone,. They played in the school band and the military. My mother is a gospel singer and plays piano.

I used to watch early 'American Bandstand' with an uncle and I think this is where I became inspired to write, sing and play and dreaming of learning more. We moved to Dayton where there were an abundance of good musicians, singers and performers in gospel, blues, r&b and jazz. It was like a mini Motown. Many major r&b and gospel stars would come to Dayton to perform and take many of the baddest musicians from there .It's the home of 'The Ohio Players', Lakeside',' Roger Troutman' & Zapp' and a few others.

My first journeyman work were with local gospel singing groups like 'The Gospel Travelers', 'The Zion Trumpeteers', 'The Wondering Souls' and a few more. I recorded with the groups and traveled throughout the United States. .

My first blues gig came with local bluesman and recording star 'Piney Brown'. I had my first band at seventeen called 'The Ohio Twisters' which performed locally until it was time for me to enter the Army. I played and performed in the military stateside and in Germany where I had a band on post and a German band off post. While in Germany I performed at the officers parties and dances, where if I was on guard duty or KP, I was asked to be relived to perform these functions which I enjoyed very much. I also toured with the German band when on leave for two or three weeks and traveled and performed USO shows.

I played guitar for Dottie Peoples, a jazz/r&b singer and now a gospel star from Dayton, who now resides in Georgia. In 1974 I was asked to join the popular group 'Heat Wave' and travel to London, England with some other friends from home. I met and knew 'Keith Wilder' now lead singer from him standing in for 'Dottie' during a time of flu. He approached me weeks later and said that his brother was flying in from London and wanted to hire guys from Dayton to join the group. At the time I was afraid to travel so far away and passed on the offer.

I worked for another great band and bandleader 'Big Jay Bush' and 'The Houserockers' who performed all over the Midwest and had a few recordings in his past. The band at that time was as good and busy as the early 'Ohio Players'. The next year feb.1975 I moved to Los Angeles, which my father had mentioned to me years earlier about doing so in 71',72' to Los Angeles to play for bigger money and bigger bands.

I used to perform a few of singer 'Bobby Womack's' and thought about moving to Los Angeles and contacting him through his record label to his office and set up an audition when I get to Los Angeles. I moved to Pasadena, Ca. to stay with my aunt and after being here a few weeks I called Bobby's office and they mailed me some of Bobby's recordings to learn. After going to Bobby's office his PR. man told me that he was out of town and wouldn't be back for a few weeks. He also told me that 'Barry Whites' office was in the same office building band that he heard that he was also looking for guitarist and I should go down and talk to them. He said that 'Sammy Davis Jr.'s' office and 'Burt Sugarman's office, the guy who handles 'Wolfman Jack'. I went down to Barry's office told them that I played guitar, I just moved there from Ohio and I wanted to audition. hey sent me to their rehearsal studio on Sunset Boulevard, where I auditioned for Barry's music conductor. I passed his audition.

The band members liked me and wanted me to get the job, so I moved in with the band and they taught me and drilled me through Barry's music listening to tapes, records and briefly learning to read some of his sheet music. I met Barry at 'The Soul Train' TV. studios and two weeks later I auditioned for Barry in Glendale,Ca. at Whitney Recording Studios' where Barry recorded his earlier hits. Barry auditioned me and a Jewish guitarist named Andy Kastner aka 'Andy Gump'.We were both hired.

In about six weeks I was touring 10,000 seaters in countries i never thought I would perform in like Munich, Brussels, London, Paris, Denmark, Amsterdam many more plus South American cities like Caracas, Bogot?, San Juan, Buenos Aries, Lima, Santo Domingo and many more. I toured with Barry White from 1975 -1979 and returned again from 1986-1990.

After leaving Barry in 79' I worked with 'Wolfman Jack'tv/radio host and celebrity on an album and later a TV show called 'The Wolfman Jack Radio Show' where I worked with the house band. On this show was when I backed 'Chuck Berry' and 'Maureen Mc Govern'on the show.A fter my time with 'Wolfman Jack' I did some extra work in 'The Blues Brother Movie' and saw Willie Hall' on the movie set who was playing drums in the movie so I spoke with him on break and I told him that I wasn't doing anything with Barry at the moment and I asked f he could get me a hook up with 'Isaac Hayes' and the next days shooting he gave me a number to call in Atlanta. It was Isaac's friend and body guard. We spoke I told him about myself. He said send a tape of me playing. A week later I received 'Isaac's' newest album to learn and two weeks. Later I received a ticket to Atlanta, Ga. where my father's people live.

I recorded on a 'Linda Clifford' album called 'I'm Yours' which 'Isaac' was producing the music and 'Curtis Mayfield' was writing the lyrics. Also in 1980' 'Isaac's' band backed a group called 'Odessys' for a record company party. During the years from September 1981-to April 84' I lived in London and I started working for a reggae group called 'The Chosen Few' who had reggae hits in the seventies in the UK.,Canada and Jamaica. I was the bandleader for the group and on my first gig 'Clem Curtis' of 'The Foundations' seen me and asked who I was and was introduced on break and was asked to work with them and soon I was working with both groups.

Sometimes i would take a taxi to the next gig, because the gigs were an hour to ninety minutes and usually at different times. I backed and performed on many reggae shows. 'The Foundations' worked more often than the other group which made it a liitle easier.

I 've toured all over England, Wales, Scotland, 'The Isle of Wight' and 'The Isle of Man'. I was on TV with 'The Foundations' on a show called 'Unforgettable' a show of old pop and soul stars from the sixties and seventies. I later met DJ./producer Tony Williams' who hired me for a session to record a song, the song 'It's Over', which went to the top of the British charts for weeks until we were called to appear on the pop tv show 'Top of the Pops'.

There was no band called 'The Funkmasters' only a name until we as session players came together as a band now called 'The Funkmasters'. The band did a little until my immigration problems of not having working papers and was deported a month later even while my music and a solo single of mine was on the radio and in the charts. After two months I was back in the states. I went back to Dayton where nothing was happening for a few months.

I got bored and decided to join an agency out of Minnesota to get me some work. Later I toured with an Italian singer from Syracuse named 'The David Julian Experience', which was a four piece band with dancers like a small Vegas act. We played r&b/soul/jazz and show tunes. We traveled to five cities in Canada and parts of America I had never been in like Fargo, ND.

In 90' I went back to the UK. and started working again with 'Clem Curtis' & The Foundations'. I met a new crop of Foundations the second time around and became good friends with the guys, especially my best friend and drummer Graham Cuttill. From 90' until 95' I was over in London every year from six weeks to six months.

In 94' I recorded my smooth jazz album 'In The Zone' with my friends from 'The Foundations' group and in 95' I took my mother to London and Paris for her birthday and one of my songs was on the radio. Also there was a friend of mine who sang with 'Chaka Khan' who was over doing a TV show with 'Jazzy B' from 'Soul To Soul'. In 95' my album was released and later that year we debuted 'In The Zone' at London's famous 'Jazz Cafe' in 'Camdentown' where many a star performed.

In 98' I met 'Freddy Couseart' the guy took in 'Marvin Gaye' when he had cocaine problems in Oostende, Belgium and who produced his last album 'Sexual Healing'. I signed a contract with Freddy and he said when I'm over he would book me, so I went to London and he booked me in Palermo, Sicily in June of 98' and he introduced me to bandleader Pete Lamont of 'The Atlantic Soul Machine' whose been around for twenty years recording and backing old r&b artist like 'Wilson Pickett',' Rufus Thomas',' Fred Wesley of 'The JB's'.

Freddy later died from a bike accident while back at home in Oostende and shortly afterwards the band asked me to join their band and become their guitarist and front singer and I accepted because they were my backing band in Sicily. I have toured Holland, belgium, UK., Helsinki and Sicily with 'The Atlantic Soul Machine'. I recorded on their last album project in 99' called 'Black, White & Blue' with all covers except the title track which the second guitarist 'Chris Staines' and I wrote called 'Black, White & Blue'. The album was released in UK. in 2000' and 2003' the states on my label 'North Star Music'/NSM Records.
