Manuel Iman - Artist Page
Manuel Iman
Booking Agency  ·  Similar Music
Biography:
 

Manuel Iman was born in Seville, Spain. He became involved in music at a very early age. His first recording came in 1975 when he made 14 de Abril with the group Goma. The album was one of the first Flamenco rock fusion efforts of that time and has now become a collector’s item. Three of the pieces of this album were included in the soundtrack of the successful film Manuela in 1976. 

In 1976 the first seeds of the progressive rock group Iman were sown and by the following year, the full band was gaining ground with its live performances. They were signed by CBS for whom they recorded Iman, Califato Independiente. The critics raved over its innovative and subtle combination of Flamenco, Rock and Eastern roots.

In 1980, Camino del Aguila was released, incorporating tropical influences. One of Manuel’s compositions was included in the soundtrack of the French film Frigg force 9.

Iman became a legendary name in Spanish rock and its recordings were re-released on CD by Musea a French record company. In 1997 Sony Music Spain reissued both albums. 

After moving to Madrid in 1981, Manuel became a sought-after creative force in the recording industry and produced, performed and arranged works for artists as varied as Lole & Manuel, Paco and Pepe de Lucia, Pata Negra, Kiko Veneno, Ole-Ole, Los del Rio, Remedios Amaya, Emilio Aragon and many more. He also worked making music for commercials and documentaries.

In 1985, Manuel got involved in the creation of a commercial project, the pop band La Decada Prodigiosa, covering 60’s songs (Emi Hispavox), that had outstanding success. The first five albums released sold 2,5 million copies and the band toured Spain extensively for five years and appeared in many TV shows. One of the biggest live shows took place in the island of Tenerife island (1989) where Manuel performed with the band in front of more than 250.000 people. He also was performing several times for the King of Spain and his Family in official and private parties along the years.

In 1991 Manuel began work on a solo album, titled La Danza del Espacio for Productora Andaluza, released in 1992.  He worked on music for the video commissioned by Tele Expo (the TV station created for the 92 World Expo) Sevilla entre dos Exposiciones. 

Manuel also coordinated and directed a large scale tour entitled Rock Andaluz- Ayer y Hoy in which important names from the past and present were brought together. 

In 1995 he moved to Malibu, California, composing and producing music for video and preparing a new album. Called from Spain in 1996 for a new project, Manuel became the Musical Director and music composer of El Circo del Arte show, a successful new project produced by the show business mogul Emilio Aragon. 

In 1997 he returned to Malibu and he has been working as a producer and composer for Dunrite Productions. In 1998 the CD Reflections was released with Domo Records, an instrumental collection that includes three pieces by Manuel. His music has also been included in Music for the Spirit, on Domo Records. Songs from a grateful heart, an instrumental Spanish guitar collection, was released in 1998 (Visions International).

For the same company, he also worked with more artists in the CD Live in Long Beach. In addition he prepared another solo album Flowers in the desert (EverSound), released in October 98, working with Grammy Award winner Roger Nichols and Grammy Award nominee Paul Dieter.  

In 1999 music by Manuel has been included in the compilation CD Expressions (EverSound) and produced and performed three carols for the compilation CD Tidings of Joy (EverSound), one of them, Little drummer boy featuring his daughter Lucia Iman.

In 2000 Manuel worked on a new album Legacy , based and inspired in old Indian bhajans creating a fusion with blues, reggae, Flamenco, rap, Brazilian and African music with musicians from around the world like Dan Moretti, Abraham Laboriel, Madan Gopal, Gita Jaiyaram, Bob Nochols, Jim Monahan, Scarlet Rivera, Nigel Holton, Windy Barnes, Sanjay Talwar and many more. Roger Nichols also mixed and mastered this project.

In 2001 Manuel produced and arranged Save the Day for Visions International, Untraveled Road, an alternative country CD for Steve Mc Peters and Rich beyond words for Rich Neel.

In 2003, Manuel released Your Love is my Blessing, an album of Latin and Flamenco music featuring his talented daughter songwriter and singer Lucia Iman. In 2007 Manuel Iman received a Special Award in the event Tribute to Andalusian Rock as a genuine pioneer of Andalusian rock. He performed with  his band music from his solo albums and recreated old hits.

The City Hall of Jerez celebrated the 30th Anniversary of the birth of the band Iman Califato Independiente, sponsoring the reunion of the band, in which Manuel was the leader and main creative force.
