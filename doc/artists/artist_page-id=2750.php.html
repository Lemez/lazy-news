Pablo "Chino" Nu?ez
Biography:
 

Pablo "Chino" N??ez is a percussionist, pianist, arranger, conductor, composer, producer, engineer, and educator. He is a well respected and sought freelance producer and percussionist. He has performed in a broad arena of venues.

By age 12, Chino began to establish his mark in the industry and was performing regularly with local talents Amaury y su T?pica Latina, Conjunto Acere, Orquesta Realidad and Ceasar y la R?tmica; while simultaneously performing with such legendary unforgettable's as Celia Cruz, Johnny Pacheco, Adalberto Santiago, Tito Puente, Ismael Miranda, Cheo Feliciano, Luis Perico Ortiz and many more.

At age 16, Chino made his first recording with who was than Johnny Ortiz y Su Sonido Taibori with Tito Nieves on vocals. This was also Tito's first recording and till this day Chino has recorded on most of his projects. But his lifetime discography library is extensive. His productions and performances have had no boundaries, or limitations and also include the likes of Missy Elliott, Jennifer Lopez, and Cindy Lauper.

Chino is the timbalero for Spanish Harlem Orchestra, whose classic old school style of music has earned them worldwide recognition in less than 3 years. Unbeknown to many, Chino also enjoys dancing. While Spanish Harlem Orchestra's vocalist Willie Torres and Marco Bermudes take over on percussion demonstrating their hidden talents, Chino and fellow- percussionist, and long-time friend, Bobbie Allende demonstrate their dance techniques.

Chino is also the musical director for the legendary Ray Sep?lveda and the popular Johnny Rivera. He is also frequently called upon to perform by the East Coast's Who's Who including Jimmy Bosch, Frankie Vasquez y Los Soneros del Barrio, Ernie Acevedo y su Conjunto Imagen, Rafael De Jes?s y su Orquesta and Conjunto Cl?sico. Chino will oftentimes receive the call of duty from the major league touring the tri-states, such as the likes of El Gran Combo De Puerto Rico, Tito Nieves, Oscar DeLe?n, Tito Rojas, Gilberto Santa Rosa, Victor Manuelle, Jerry Rivera, Rub?n Blades, Michael Stewart and many more.

When he's not performing, Chino can be found at his home recording studio, Jan-Mar Productions. Meticulously and methodically composing and arranging the unique and rhythmic swing projects that he is distinctively known for.

Personally, Chino has many interests and is supportive of the Arts through community-based initiatives. He promotes positive example through music with organizations such as Music Against Drugs and the Boys & Girls Harbor of Performing Arts. He is also passionate about his involvement with inner-city children and has sponsored little league softball teams and volunteers at youth-oriented functions. In the past, he has directed the Ray Sepulveda Orquesta in numerous performances at the Yankee Stadium for World Series and ACLS games, as well as other MLB events.

Chino has received countless lifetime achievement awards. As a member of the Spanish Harlem Orchestra, he was nominated for a Grammy Award in 2002, won a Latin Billboard Award in 2003, has been nominated for a 2005 Latin Billboard Award and by the International Reggae & World Music Awards for Best Salsa/Latin Oriented Entertainer of 2004. But perhaps the most prestigious recognition yet was on February 13, 2005, when Spanish Harlem Orchestra received a Grammy for Best Salsa Album of 2004.

In his lifetime, Chino has been fortunate to have toured extensively from coast to coast and abroad, currently with the Spanish Harlem Orchestra. However, most memorable were his performances with the legendary's Hector Lavoe, Willie Col?n, Ray Barretto, and many others whose music he absorbed and also considers instrumental to his success today. He has also engaged in numerous Broadway Show projects including Evita, The Cape Man and Lion King, and occasionally will play the shows. In addition, he has made an abundance of videos; and had a cameo role with Adalberto Santiago & His Orchestra in a major motion picture entitled Moscow on the Hudson starring Robin Williams and Maria Conchita Alonzo.

Chino has an upcoming CD titled, "It's SHOtime, Strictly Hardcore On 1 or 2", Un Homenaje Al Bailador, A Tribute to Dancers, on the Cookita Records label.
