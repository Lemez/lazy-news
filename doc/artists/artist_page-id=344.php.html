Los Manolos - Artist Page
Los Manolos
Discography  ·  Booking Agency  ·  Similar Music
Biography:
 

The Los Manolos story began in the summer of 1990 when a group of friends, ten to be exact, from different rock bands, got together to play Catalan Rumba with a rock attitude. They started playing on the local club circuit and got booked for an ever increasing number of exclusive private parties. Wearing outrageous, out of fashion suits and playing their particular style of Rumba, their show earned them a great deal of attention. So much that they recorded a music video of their version of All My Loving for MTV. Soon after that, the band signed with RCA (BMG).

In the spring of '91 they recorded their first album Pasión Condal, produced by Julio Palacios, in Madrid at Sincronía Studios. At the same time they composed and recorded the music for Un Cel de Sorra for the Dau Rodó Theatre Company. In the Summer of the same year their version of Lennon and McCartney's All My Loving got to Nº3 in the charts while the album was the second best selling record of the year. They played an 80 date tour of Spain with a peak audience in Barcelona of 90,000 and become the first Catalan group to go quadruple platinum with their first album. Pasión Condal was released in more than 20 countries including. That Summer they record their second album, Dulce Veneno (Sweet Poison).
In the Summer of '92 they set off on a 90 date tour of Spain. They played at the closing ceremony for the 1992 Olympic Games in Barcelona, doing the same for the Para-Olympics and later played at the opening of the Special Olympics. They recorded their version of Amigos Para Siempre, the Olympic Anthem, and got to Nº3 in the national music sales charts leaving for a promotional tour of Argentina and Chile that winter.
1993 saw changes in the group when four of the members left. The sextet of original members inaugurated the disco Up &Down in Cancún (Mexico), traveled to London to play at the Equinox, toured Portugal and Switzerland and later that year were signed by Horus Records. The summer sees the group touring once again, 40 dates, macro-concerts, film premiers etc.

They recorded their 3rd record Bailes Regionales, produced by Josep Mas, in Barcelona in 1994. After the presentation in Sala Zeleste and a short tour of Germany they came back to tour Spain and Portugal. They composed and recorded the theme tune for the television series A La Fresca and appeared many times on this popular program.

In 1995 the group formed a coop named Ventilador Music (Record Company, Studio, Backline Service, Management and Production.) as a platform for new groups in the Roots Music scene (Flamenco, Rumba, Folk, Ethnic.). That Summer they toured Spain and Portugal.

In 1997 they returned to the studio to record Robando Gallinas (Stealing Chickens) their fourth album. The song Continuará was chosen as the theme song for a program of the same name on Televisión Española. Since then they have been familiar guests and headliners for many programs and macro-concerts and continue composing for television and theatre.
Los Manolos are:
Carles Lordan - Bass, Vocals
Andreu Hernández - Drums, Percussion
Toni Pelegrín - Guitar, Vocals
Josep Gómez - Guitar, Vocals
