Jide Chord - Artist Page
Jide Chord
Discography  ·  Interview  ·  Booking Agency  ·  Similar Music
Biography:
 

Jide Chord is a seasoned artist who after having toured extensively in West Africa came to live in the UK in 1988. He formed a new band 3 years later in the summer of 1991. He has since then performed around the globe to wide acclaim.

Jide Chord and his ten-piece band play Juju music, which comes originally from southern Nigeria. This music, made popular by artists like King Sunny Ade, the late I.K. Dairo, Ebenezer Obey & Sir Shina Peters, makes a clever fusion of traditional drumming with modern instruments like guitars, synthesizers & Saxophones. However, Jide has created his own unique sound. He produces a rootsy, vibrant sound that is in line with the Yoruba peoples zest for life. With the emphasis on singing and drumming, his music creates a trance-like reaction from his audience whenever he performs.

While he is an accomplished songwriter, Jide draws from an infinite repertoire of Yoruba folklore. From his original Juju Music point of view, Jide Chord imbibes Afro beat & Highlife to create his own unique form of African music. His stage act is very colorful and easy to enjoy while taking you on a musical journey in Africa.

Jide enjoys superstar status within his Nigerian community at home, Europe, the United States, and the UK. His reputable band is a collection of highly versatile musicians, dancers, writers and arrangers producing a show usually performed by up to 20 people. He uses new technology a lot while retaining all the dynamics, colour and texture of a truly African show.

Apart from playing week in week out at functions within the Nigerian expatriate community, he has in the recent past performed at many festivals and venues including; The Fridge-Brixton, Nottingham Riverside Festival, Africa Centre Covent Garden, Ronnie Scotts, Carlton Television, Ealing International Jazz Festival, The Buttermarket Shrewsbury, Malcolm X Centre Bristol, Warwick University Coventry, Lewisham Peoples Day and Leeds-Talking Reality to name a few.

While based in the UK, he has kept close to the music scene in Nigeria thereby affording himself regular appearances both in Nigeria & his adopted home, UK. Outside of these two countries, he has also visited, France, Belgium, Switzerland, Holland, Germany, Italy, the USA, Sweden, Canada, Ivory Coast & Japan.

Born Babajide Akinwunmi into Yoruba land in the heart of Africa, Jide comes from a long line of musicians. His family tree is dotted with notable musicians from historical times. His maternal grand-father was in his lifetime a famous church musician. Jide started performing publicly at the tender age of five. According to him music comes naturally.

Jide is also the brain behind the internationally acclaimed acapella group The Kings of Lagos, who have performed in front of royalty and are referred to by critics as West Africa's answer to Ladysmith Black Mamabazo.

Interview:
 

Jide Chord

Compact, grinning, and full of energy, Jide Chord explains how he acquired his stage name "When I started doing music, I used to be very particular about which chords were played to my songs - my mates used to say 'Here comes Mr.Chord, he is always asking which chord it is!'" Jide was born in Nigeria and trained to be a journalist, but always secretly wanted to do music. "I think I first saw the glamour of it when I was 12 or 13, but I was hooked from my first performance at school at the age of 5. Everybody kept talking about it, but to me it just came naturally."

Jide specialises in Nigerian Juju music, a combination of traditional African music with western instruments and styles. "Juju is a link to the west. We do rock, and it has attitude, rather than the calm, serious African stuff; it's about having fun, talking about sex, dressing and dancing." And Juju has also given Jide a forum he might not otherwise have had. "I left the confines of the church choir because I believed that the medium would be wasted if I only used it in a restricted environment. I want to be able to talk about religion, love, nature, motherhood, politics."

Jide says that he came to the UK in 1988 because "I was finding it difficult to do the things I wanted to do - I knew that in a well-structured environment I could maximise my potential." He was right. Now a leader of three bands - Jide Chord's own 10 piece band, The Kings of Lagos and Natives Abroad - Jide is a well-known member of the Nigerian artist community. "Ultimately I want to be one of those to be reckoned with when they talk about World Music." Jide Chord knows he stands a good chance of being just that.
