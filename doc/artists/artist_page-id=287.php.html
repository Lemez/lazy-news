Arcángel - Artist Page
Arcángel
Booking Agency  ·  Similar Music
Biography:
 

Translated by Rita Granda

While in his early twenties, Arcángel became part of a select group of flamenco singers. He maintains this position because he wants to, because he can and because he knows how to do it.

Francisco José Arcángel Ramos was born in Huelva in 1977. His family was originally from the town of Alosno, known as the birthplace of fandango and the main source of the province’s rich folklore. At a mere 10 years of age, Arcángel demonstrated an unprecedented command of the local fandango when he took First Prize in a children’s competition organized by a flamenco association in Huelva called “La Orden” (“The Order”). This trophy was the prelude to a long series of honours that would eventually encourage Arcángel to dedicate himself professionally to his passion for singing flamenco.

Arcángel is capable of maintaining control of his singing technique, including compás, voice tuning and musical structure, while broadcasting an endless reverberation of perfectly rounded tones. That’s how you connect! And we’re not talking about his influence in the feminine arena, which we know nothing about, but to his amazing ability to present a cante (flamenco song) in one go, at a slow radiant pace while barely taking a breath.  

Without a doubt, he has an Olympic temperament, comparable to the bullfighter’s ability to ‘moderate, adjust and control’ a charging bull.  One of Arcángel’s outstanding characteristics is the intelligent way that he updates the traditional styles from his homeland.  He revives them by adding infinite melodic overtones and by minimizing constraints. 

Do you remember that weak flamenco song that was forced upon us years ago by ‘The Stars of the Convoluted Tune’? Well, Arcángel is nothing like that. His easy tone doesn’t lack any of flamenco’s necessary tension just the opposite, it is full of tension. Arcángel possesses an unprecedented knowledge of the great styles in flamenco and he can critique each in great detail. He moves us, without shrillness, by highlighting the truth of the flamenco singer. His current look confirms that he is young in both years and spirit but as an artist this youthfulness makes him open to today’s trends. And cantar (flamenco singing), what is known as cantar, is just that drinking from yesterday yet sounding like today in order to have meaning tomorrow. Arcángel makes this clear in his singing:              

Yes to today’s flamenco,

yes to the old flamenco   

I know its roots

as well as what’s happening right now

and I have just one thing to say,

who ever has it understands it,

we haven’t gone crazy.

No one needs to give me advice

I can make my own mistakes.           

What mistakes? I know I’m going to get in trouble for this but, having studied it, I declare that Arcángel’s cante is RH+: resounding, handsome, and on the up and up. The first one to realize this was the guitarist Juan Carlos Romero: “I listened to him one night in a bar in Huelva, which is our home town, and I thought he had possibilities. I told him to come with me, and he started to sing in my recitals. He progressed continuously”. And how. Arcángel caused quite a stir at the X Flamenco Biennial, celebrated in Seville in 1998.  Since the dancers, like Yerbabuena, had already chosen Arcángel as one of their favourite flamenco singers, his presence on the conference stages was continuous. Nevertheless, it was in Juan Carlos Romero’s concert, Abanaó, that he captivated the audience once and for all.

The proud city in Seville didn’t hesitate to rave about the triumph of such a young flamenco singer. Without a doubt, that show was clearly decisive for his initial season. “Some of the themes that he composed for that day are recorded on this disk”, recalls Juan Carlos.  There is brotherhood from Huelva in this work. Understandably, Arcángel wanted to make his debut in the recording world on the skilled arm of his “brother”, Juan Carlos Romero, who became the producer and main author of the work presented here. “A fundamental philosophy exists in the flamenco concept of a recording. It’s not about performing ‘nice’ songs we are searching for an understanding of the music and art. Arcángel has matured during the time that we have worked together, and that is what this recording shows.”  

It truly is a great pleasure to welcome the first album of this enchanting young flamenco singer whose reputation in flamenco is real, and therefore, global. We end with the comment made by Carmen Linares after a superb recital by Arcángel at the Medical School in Madrid: “Who said it was over?”
