Beatriz Martín - Artist Page
Beatriz Martín
Booking Agency  ·  Similar Music
Biography:
 

She began dancing at the age of 9 with the teachers Angelita and Charo Romero in Granada. Her first appearance as a professional was in 1984 at the Dance and Music International Festival of Granada when she was just 14.

She performed for the first time in Madrid at Albeniz Theatre in 1985 on Rafael Aguilar Company, in the play "The House of Bernarda Alba" by Lorca, where she was the first dancer. With this company she did her first international tours, to events such as Versiliana Festival (Italy), The Divines, together with figures such as Antonio Gades, Luciana Savignano, Carla Fracci, and others.

At 15, Antonio Gades offered her to play "Carmen".

She was the guest artist in the homage to Pilar López occurred in Italy. She performed the opera "The Brief Live" on the paper of "The Gypsy" at La Fenice Theatre together with artists such as Sergi Salas, Alfredo Kraus, among others.

In 1989 she danced as soloist in the show of the guitarist Victor Monge "Serranito", in a tour to Egypt, Morocco and Turkey. In the same year she represented Spain in the Ski Olympics '91-93 in Istanbul.

In 1990 she performed as guest artist in the show "Stars of the Dance", with Maya Plisetskaya. In this year she took part in the "I Muestra Joven de Flamenco" of Malaga. She also took part as first dancer in Luisillo's Company at the Cannes Dance Festival.

In 1991 she took part with other artists in a show in New York, as part of the events connecting the Liberty Statue of NY and the statue of Chistopher Columbus, in Barcelona. She also participated in the "II Muestra Joven de Flamenco", this time at the Roman Amphitheatre of Merida. This year she also performed in the Biennial of Seville in the show "Candela".

In 1992 she began as first dancer on the Company of the master Mario Maya, in the play "3 Flamenco Movements" making pair with him in "Amargo" and "El Amor Brujo". She also took part in different festivals in Spain and toured Japan.

She appeared as soloist in El Güito's and Manolete's Company with whom she has made different festivals among others "Los Veranos de la Villa" (Madrid), "Lille Festival" and "Nimes Festival".

In 1993 she took part in Arriaga Theatre Festivals in Bilbao with El Güito's Company. She also participated in the Dance and Music International Festivals of Granada with the work "Apología a Carmen Amaya", as well as in the works "Flamenco is Life" in Cordoba, Biarritz and Miami.

In January 1994 she participated in some Festivals in Istanbul, with Manolo Sanlucar. She has appeared on international and local TV programmes such as "Puerta del Cante", (Canal Sur), "Arte y Artistas Flamencos". She collaborated in the film "Nosferatu II", starred by Klaus Kinski.

In March of that year it is stablished in Andalusia the Andalusian Company of Dance in which she appeared as soloist in "De lo Flamenco" and as first dancer in "Requiem Flamenco", conducted by Mario Maya and performed for the first time at Maestranza Theatre in Seville. The same year she performed at the Biennial of Seville at Lope de Vega Theatre with "Flamenco is Life".

In 1995 the teacher and choreographer Jose Granero made a production titled "Women" for the Maestranza Theatre for the opening of "Italica Festival '95". In May this year she received the Rosario National Price "La Mejorana" in the National Contest of Cordoba. Later she works with maestro Granero again in two of his shows: "Variaciones románticas" and "Movimiento perpetuo".

In January 1996 she took part at Cemal Resit Concert Festival in Istanbul. In March she obtained an important success together with other artists in "Flamenco", a production created for the Tanz Festival in Vienna, and also represented at Centro Cultural de Belem, Lisboa, and Centro Cultural de la Villa, Madrid, including a dance with Ricardo Franco, first dancer of the Compañía Nacional de Danza and director of the show. In May of that same year she performs at the Flamenco Festival in Vredenburg, Utrecht (Netherlands), created by Centro Flamenco Puro.

She participates in the work "El amor brujo" as Candela created for the Festival Internacional de Música y Danza de Granada, and co-produced by the IX Bienal de Sevilla, choreography of Manolo Marín. She also presents for the first time the show "Lances del arenal" in this Bienal, at the UNESCO International Gala, created by Cristina Heeren Flamenco Art Foundation.

At the present she is performing as first dancer in the show "Flamenco y Fado"
