Magda Angelica - Artist Page
Magda Angelica
Discography  ·  Interview  ·  Booking Agency  ·  Similar Music
Biography:
 

Magda Angelica was born in Guatemala, Central America, and her childhood was formed by two mayan women of the cackchiquel ethnic group.

Since she was a little girl, Magda Angelica had a deep interest in music and art, so she started to learn vocal technique, theatre, and composition. Her work in music, started in studio with a live presentation.

In 1996, recorded her first album Inner Garden (Jard?n interior). This record included compositions by Magda Angelica and other Guatemalan artists. For this album, she was honored with prizes given by important institutions in her country.

In 2001, recorded her second Album Clay Angels (Angeles de Barro). All of the songs are written by Magda Angelica and talk about her vision about daily life themes.

Changing her music style, in 2005, Magda Angelica created her third album Dream Weaver (Tejedora de Sue?os). All the lyrics are based on the mayan and mexican spirituality with a fusion of electronic and prehispanic sounds.

Official Web Site: www.magdangelica.com

Interview:
 
Magda Angelica Interview Questions

1. Please will you tell me more about your background in your own words --
where you grew up, how you got into music, what kind of musical training you got, etc. I'm looking for something substantially different and more in depth than what's out there already. And I'll punctuate it with further questions to make a good read.


I was born and grew up in Guatemala, in Central America. I had the gift of growing up with two Mayan women. They taught me another sense of life, different values and a special cosmovission.

Since I was a little girl, I knew that music will be the must important element in my life. I started with vocal technique classes, and recorded jingles and back vocals.

Then, in 1996, I recorded my first album ?Jardin Interior? (Inner Garden) and in 2001, the second album ??ngeles de Barro? (Clay Angels) was released.

I moved to M?xico in 2002.

This has been a very important experience, because I founded a lot of answers to questions that I had mainly about the Indian spirituality. I met a group of people that work of the sacred stone ?Obsidiana?. Through the Obsidiana they guide a deep contact with the inner being.

With these work, I discovered another way of making music and to integrate my childhood experiences with the wisdom of this two Mayan women, and the mysticism of the Mexican people. So, the latest album was born: ?Tejedora de Sue?os? (Dreams Weaver)

2. Were there any projects you worked on prior to your latest album? What
were they like?

At the beginning, I worked with Guatemalan artists, recording jingles and doing back vocals. It was a good experience, because recording in a studio, gave me a very good training.

Then, I started to write my own songs.

My first album, was my first experiment with music. It was a pop album. I had the opportunity to open concerts to Laura Paussini, Alberto Cortez, Jos? Jos?, Raul Diblassio, Armando Manzanero and Jorge Mu?iz.

Even I had good results, I felt a lack of a deep contact with the audiences. It was like stoping before reaching their souls. This was the reason I started to seek inside of me for another kind of music.

At this time, I had a lot of people working with me: musicians, singers, dancers, etc., but I wasn?t happy with the results, because even that everything was carefully planned, the deep contact with the people, was still far away.

So, with another friend (a musician and composer who plays the cello), we made a series of concerts that was called ?The Nude Song?, were the lyrics had the main role. The challenge was to guide people through a jorney between the contrast of different emotions. It was a satisfying experience.

Then I started to work in my second album: ??ngeles de Barro?. In this album, all the songs are written by me and I talk about the vision of my reality at that moment. A female point of view of things that happened around me.

Finally, I had a deep change. I felt more free to write music in order to go deeply into the indian spirituality, and ?Tejedora de Sue?os? (Dreams Weaver), my latest album, was born. I think that I found my own way to make music, with more freedom and honesty.
In M?xico, I have been invited to perform with another musicians in live concerts, like Fratta.

I also did some musical theater too.

3. What can you tell us about other artists that have worked with you on your earlier projects?


I started with Jorge Estrada, a musician called and also owner of a studio recording jingles, then I worked with the most important rock band in Central America: Alux Nahual, in their live presentations and the drummer ?Lenin Fern?ndez?, started to work with me in my recordings.

?Fernando Scheel?, another Guatemalan composer, made all the electronic musical arrangements of my latest album, but in the two first recordings, he was a very important part of my team.

Two special women are Linda and Calixta Gabriel. They are Mayan people. Linda from Quich? and Calixta from Cakchiquel ethnic groups.

Linda colaborated with me singing in ?Nanita?, and Calixta gave me the lyric of a pray that Cakchiquel woman make the day that they initiated as spiritual guides in their community. She is the voice in the pray of the song ?Belej?` B?atz?. Both are songs of my latest album.

Carlos Chaclan a researcher of pre-Hispanic instruments. He is ceramist and musician too. He builds instruments with ancient designs. He recorded this instruments in my latest album.

I had worked also with musicians from Mexico and Costa Rica, like Manuel Obregon, director of the international Papaya Orchestra, whom has included one of my themes in their latest album: ?Tierra de la Dulce Espera?.

4. Where do the album names come from? Is there anything specific you are trying to connote?

?Inner Garden?. I gave this name to the record because, even it was a pop album, since I started in music, it was very important for me to ?say something? with the songs. Say something important. So, Inner Garden is a deep space in my self, that I wanted to show trough music.

The second album ?Clay Angels?. This name is because I believe that this is the nature of human beings. We have the ability to fly and reach higher grounds, but sometimes, our inner chains stop us in this journey. Our fears, doubts, guilts, etc, stop us. This is the clay?

The third album ?Dream Weaver?, is because the meaning of the weaving in the Indian spirituality.

They believe that the Moon (the grandmother) taught to women the art of waving.
All this art is a tribute to the miracle of creating life.

All the human beings have the power to create or destroy life.

We have the choice, but when we are weaving dreams, we are building something beautiful and a blessing to our world.

5. What kind of music--or artists perhaps--has influenced your own?

In my latest album, the ethnic music from Guatemala and Mexico. Since I was a little girl, I heard this music played with marimba. The rhythm called ?The son guatemalteco? is made with the 6/8 measure. Is like the rhythm of the human heart.

I think that music of contemporary artists, like Enya, Mecano, Allanise Morrisette has influenced me also.

6. Tell us about the music you listen to listened to before it all started and what you are currently playing all the time. 

I like to hear different music styles. Rock, jazz, experimental music, ethnic, etc. And specially the one composed by women.

Nowadays, I am more into the world music.

7. How would you characterize your music today? And tell us more about your vocal work - how did you develop your voice, the various styles, andwhere do the inspirations for your work come from?

My music today is a fusion of experiences and life, I talk about the wonderful things that I found in the Indian mysticism. Is a gift for me because they think more freely and deeply. The two forces: male and female are necessary to give life. So God is not only a man, God is a man and a woman, father and mother at the same time.

I have been working on vocal techniques since I was 13 years old. I'm constantly seeking to found new elements to improve my style.

8. What has the reaction to your albums been?

All the albums had a good comments, but in the latest, I feel that people is traveling with me to their deep inner being, discovering new emotions and experiences of life.

9. Can you tell us about the others that work with you on your latest project?

Calixta and Linda, the two mayan women who I talked about.

Lenin Fernandez, who plays clay percussion instruments. He called them ?tonajas?, because they produce musical tones. Fernando Scheel: he made all the electronic arrangements. Jenifer Pinto: back vocals
From Mexico: Sr. Gonzalez: He produced the album with me and record some electronic percussions. Leo Granados: Made the mixes. Felipe Kobeh: Made the mastering.

10. Is there a story you are trying to convey in the album or in any of the individual tracks? Can you elaborate on it please. 

Some of the themes, are based on legends and stories that Indian people tell in their communities. In some others, I talk about magical beings, spirituality, and beliefs.

11. Can you tell us how the other performers were selected and a little bit
about your experiences working with them.

All the musicians were selected because there experience working with pre-Hispanic instruments, except Fernando Scheell. I selected him because he understood my intension in the making of this album. He understood the mystic atmosphere that I wanted to achieve.

It is a wonderful experience to work with all this people, all are friends and very good musicians.

12. Are there any songs on the album that particularly that stick out for you? And why?

I think ?Nanita?. It's a song written to the two Mayan women that I grew up with. I think that they are like trees. All the time are standing up with a very deep roots, with a big wisdom and a grate love. Nanita is a tribute for them.

13. Do you plan any live performances? And if you've been out there already, what has it been like for you?

We are already making live presentations in Mexico. It's a great experience because people come with me to talk about life experiences.

All the time, new people are coming to the concerts and that makes me very happy.

14. Do you think the internet will help your musical career?

Of course. I think that internet is a very important tool to diffuse music and to shorten time and distances. I think internet will be the most important media to distribute and promote music in the near future.

15. Do you have a day job that our readers might be interested in hearing about? Maybe tell us about some of the things you do.

I'm working the Obsidiana therapy with people. Is a period of three months, making a deep contact with the inner being. Also, I'm studying mexica philosophy and other kind of alternative therapies.

I'm working with children, teaching music.

16. What are your hopes plans and dreams for the rest of 2006?

I am working on a new album writing new songs. This new album is going to be more in to the tribal style. I hope to be recording during the second half of the year.
