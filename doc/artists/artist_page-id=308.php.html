Cristina Pato - Artist Page
Cristina Pato
Similar Music
Biography:
 

Cristina Pato was one of the youngest bagpipers that came out of the wave of Spanish bagpipers in the late 1990s. At the age of 18, the young Galician piper recorded her first solo album. Her modern grungy look with dyed green and red hair was quite a contrast to the look of traditional bagpipers with folksy costumes.

Born in Orense in 1980, Pato picked up a bagpipe for the first time at the age of 4. Her parents had her take bagpipe lessons at a bagpipe school. At the age of 6, she was admitted to the conservatory, where she also learned piano.

Pato studied piano at the Barcelona conservatory. "With a piano it is easier to express yourself. It is an instrument with many resources. However, with a bagpipe you can only play a melody. Playing a bagpipe is a matter of performance rather than instrumentation, you have to have something special."

Despite her young age, Pato was an experienced musician. At a young age she was already touring Europe and north American with a famous bagpipe band from Galicia, the Banda de Gaitas de Ourense. She also recorded two albums with her band Mutenrohi.

For the recording of Tolemia (1998), Pato was able to recruit very well known musicians from the Spanish folk music scene, including Carlos Castro, Fia Na Roca’s percussionist Paco Juncal,  Berroguetto’s former violinist and there are also members from her old band Mutenrohi. Pato ventured into world music by fusing Galician Celtic music with African and southern Spanish sounds.

On her second album, Xilento, Pato explored the connections between Galicia and Portugal, its neighbor to the south. The recording features several members of Madredeus, the legendary Portuguese band.

Cristina Pato currently enjoys an active career devoted to both Galician popular and classical music. Her dual careers have led to major performances throughout the world, including appearances at New York’s Carnegie Hall and Lincoln Center, Chicago’s Symphony Hall, Portugal’s Festa do Avante in Lisbon, Glasgow’s Celtic Connections, San Marino’s Etnofestival, Brazil’s Liszt Festival in Rio de Janeiro, as well as festivals throughout Europe, India, Mexico and her native Spain. She has collaborated with international artists such as Yo-Yo Ma, The Chieftains, Spanish piper and electronic bagpipe inventor Hevia, the Tenerife and Galicia Symphony Orchestras, the Royal Pipe Band, and the Silk Road Ensemble with the Chicago Symphony Orchestra conducted by Miguel Harth-Bedoya.

Cristina Pato has performed several world premieres, including Osvaldo Golijov’s From Air to Air with the Silk Road Ensemble and Yo-Yo Ma and the 2007 premiere of his Rose of the Winds; Octavio Vazquez’s Piano Concerto with Orquesta Sinfonica de Galicia; and Montserrat Torras’s Norte, which was dedicated to Cristina. She also produced, composed and performed the soundtrack of the 2007 Spanish film El Hombre de Arena (Sony Pictures, Iroko Films, Spain).

In addition to multiple solo gaita recordings, Cristina Pato has recorded albums as a collaborative pianist and has appeared on more than twenty recordings as a guest artist, including the Grammy award winning Yo-Yo Ma and Friends; Songs of Joy and Peace (Sony BMG 2008), Silk Road Ensemble's Off the Map (2009) and Miles Español (2010).

A Doctor of Musical Arts recipient in Collaborative Piano from the Mason Gross School of the Arts at Rutgers University, Cristina Pato holds multiple degrees, including Master of Music Degrees in Piano Performance, Music Theory and Chamber Music from the Conservatorio de Musica del Liceu in Barcelona and a Master of Fine Arts Degree in Digital Arts (Computer Music) from the Universidad Pompeu Fabra in Barcelona. Her final Digital Arts project, a multi-media piece titled Ansiedad, was displayed at the international art festival Barcelona Arte Contemporaneo in 2003.

Cristina Pato currently resides in New York City.
